GoBean makes single-use packaging sustainable. Used coffee grounds are collected from cafés and pressed into 100% compostable cup sleeves — locally collected, ergonomically designed, part of the circular economy.

Scroll to discover

Billions of takeaway cups are used every year...

Every minute, 5,300 disposable hot drink cups are used in Germany alone. This equates to 2.8 billion cups of waste per year.

↓

Every coffee-to-go produces around 11 grams of coffee grounds

Multiplied by 2.8 billion cups a year, that's over 30,000 tonnes of coffee-grounds discarded annually.

↓

GoBean uses this waste to make something new...

Not another reusable cup — but the little ring around your hot drink that keeps your fingers from burning: a cup-sleeve!

The Result

The Bean-Sleeve

It is made from 100% biodegradable material. It has a shelf life of up to one year when dry. Once composted, it breaks down within three to four weeks.

The Concept

From Waste to Product

GoBean reframes the relationship between businesses, consumers, and waste: Cafés shift from waste producers to resource suppliers and customers move from passive consumers to active participants in a material cycle — composting or growing plants with used sleeves.

Circular Business Model

Waste-for-Less

GoBean integrates the circular economy directly into its business model with the 'Waste-for-Less' principle: the more waste returned by partner-cafés, the more Bean-Sleeves can be produced, the lower the unit price.
